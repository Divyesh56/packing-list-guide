Why paper is still better than apps

Phone apps are great for many things, but when your suitcase is open on the bed and your hands are full, constantly unlocking your phone screen is frustrating. A physical piece of paper lets you easily cross things off with a pen as they go into your bag.

Instead of wrestling with Excel or Word templates, using a tool that exports directly to PDF ensures your list fits perfectly on standard A4 paper. You get a clean layout every time without adjusting any page settings.

Planning for short trips vs. long trips

Your packing needs change based on how long you will be away. A quick weekend getaway requires a very different approach than a two-week vacation.

Packing for a week or more

If you are traveling for a full week, you have to think about laundry and repeating outfits. You don't need seven of everything. A good list allows you to group things logically—separating your daily essentials from items you only need once, like a heavy jacket or specific formal shoes.

Packing for a 3 or 4-day trip

For a short 3-day trip, the goal is usually to fit everything into a single carry-on bag. Space is tight. By making a custom list, you can focus strictly on the basics. You can create a section just for your airport liquids and a section for your electronics, keeping your packing process fast and light.

Customizing for your personal needs

Generic travel lists you find online are often full of things you don't need. They might have a section for "camping gear" when you are staying in a hotel. Also, a list for a solo male traveler will look very different from a list for a mother traveling with toddlers.

By using an empty template that you fill in yourself, you avoid the clutter. You only see the items that matter to your specific trip.
